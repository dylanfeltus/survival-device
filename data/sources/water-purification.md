# Water Purification and Procurement

*Original content based on US Army FM 21-76/FM 3-05.70 principles (public domain)*

## Water Procurement

The human body can survive weeks without food but only days without water. In survival situations, finding and purifying water is your highest priority after immediate shelter and medical needs.

### Finding Water Sources

**Natural Sources:**
- Streams and rivers (preferably moving water)
- Lakes and ponds (still water requires more purification)
- Springs (generally safer than surface water)
- Rain collection (cleanest natural source)
- Dew collection (use absorbent cloth on grass at dawn)
- Snow and ice (melt before consuming, never eat directly)

**Environmental Indicators:**
- Follow animal tracks (often lead to water)
- Observe bird flight patterns at dawn and dusk
- Look for green vegetation in otherwise dry areas
- Check low-lying areas and valley bottoms
- Listen for sounds of flowing water

**WARNING:** Avoid water with:
- Soapy appearance or petroleum smell
- Algae blooms (blue-green discoloration)
- Dead animals nearby
- Chemical odors
- Brackish or salty taste

## Purification Methods

All water from natural sources should be considered contaminated and must be purified before drinking.

### 1. Boiling (Most Reliable)

**Procedure:**
1. Bring water to a rolling boil
2. Maintain boil for 1 minute at sea level
3. Add 1 additional minute for every 1,000 feet above sea level
4. Allow to cool before drinking

**Effectiveness:** Kills all bacteria, viruses, and parasites. Does not remove chemical contaminants.

**Advantage:** No special equipment needed, 100% effective against biological threats.

**Disadvantage:** Requires fuel and container, does not improve taste.

### 2. Chemical Purification

**Iodine Tablets:**
- Add 2 tablets per liter of clear water
- Double dosage for cloudy water
- Wait 30 minutes before drinking (60 minutes if water is very cold)
- Shelf life: 3-5 years if properly stored

**Chlorine Bleach (Unscented, 5-6% sodium hypochlorite):**
- Add 2 drops per liter of clear water
- Double dosage for cloudy water
- Wait 30 minutes, should have slight chlorine smell
- If no smell, repeat dosage and wait another 15 minutes

**WARNING:** Do not use iodine for more than 2-3 weeks continuously. Not recommended for pregnant women or those with thyroid conditions.

### 3. Solar Still

**Construction:**
1. Dig hole approximately 3 feet wide and 2 feet deep
2. Place collection container in center
3. Cover hole with clear plastic sheet
4. Seal edges with soil and rocks
5. Place small rock in center to create cone shape directing condensation to container
6. Can add vegetation or urine around (not in) container to increase moisture

**Yield:** 1-3 pints per day depending on conditions.

**Best Use:** Desert or arid environments with intense sun.

### 4. Filtration

**Improvised Filter:**
1. Use cloth, sand, charcoal, and gravel in layers
2. Pour water through multiple times
3. **CRITICAL:** Filtration alone does NOT purify water
4. Must combine with boiling or chemical treatment

**Commercial Filters:**
- Look for filters rated to 0.2 microns or smaller
- Follow manufacturer instructions for flow rate and lifespan
- Many filters do NOT remove viruses (too small)
- Combine with chemical treatment in areas with known viral contamination

### 5. UV Purification

**Method:**
- Fill clear plastic bottle
- Place in direct sunlight for 6 hours (or 2 days if cloudy)
- UV radiation kills most pathogens

**Limitations:** Only works with clear water in clear containers. Not effective against all parasites.

## Signs of Contaminated Water

**Visual Indicators:**
- Cloudy or murky appearance
- Surface film or oil sheen
- Foam (except natural waterfall foam)
- Unusual colors (red, green, brown)

**Smell Indicators:**
- Sulfur or "rotten egg" smell
- Chemical or petroleum odor
- Strong decay smell

**Environmental Indicators:**
- Dead fish or animals in or near water
- Absence of any aquatic life or vegetation
- Mining or industrial activity upstream
- Agricultural runoff (fertilizer, pesticides)

## Water Storage

**Short-term (1-3 days):**
- Use clean, food-grade containers
- Keep covered and in shade
- Consume oldest water first

**Long-term (weeks to months):**
- Sterilize containers with boiling water first
- Add 2 drops of unscented bleach per liter
- Store in cool, dark location
- Rotate supply every 6 months
- Mark containers with purification date

## Daily Water Requirements

**Minimum Survival:** 2 liters per day
**Active/Hot Climate:** 4-6 liters per day
**Extreme Exertion:** Up to 10 liters per day

**Dehydration Signs:**
- Dark yellow urine
- Decreased urination
- Dry mouth and lips
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Rapid heartbeat
- Confusion or irritability

**Action:** Increase water intake immediately. If dehydration is severe, add pinch of salt and sugar to water to help absorption.

## Emergency Water Ration

If water is scarce:
- Reduce activity during hottest parts of day
- Breathe through nose, not mouth
- Do not eat if you have no water (digestion requires water)
- Never drink seawater, urine, or blood (accelerates dehydration)
- Avoid alcohol and caffeine

Remember: It's better to drink questionable water than to become severely dehydrated. If you must choose, drink first and purify later if immediate need is critical. Dehydration kills faster than waterborne illness.
